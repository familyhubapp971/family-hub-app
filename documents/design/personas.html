Family Hub — Personas

Four people. Each one drives different design decisions. Read the cards once, then keep this open while you design — every screen should answer "which of these four wins this trade-off?"

Names match the canonical Khan family used everywhere else in the product (dashboard mockups, signup flow, test data). Designs that use Khan-family copy stay consistent end-to-end.

S
★ Primary Admin · Mum · 38

Sarah Khan

The coordinating parent who owns the family mental load.

"I don't have time for an onboarding wizard. Just let me in, let me see what's happening, and let me close the tab."

Top jobs she hires Family Hub to do

  • See everything at a glance — kids' habits, today's tasks, this week's meals
  • Assign chores + track who did what without nagging in person
  • Settle "what's for dinner?" + "did you finish your homework?" without 5 group chats

Friction she will not tolerate

  • Multi-step onboarding wizards. Anything >3 screens loses her.
  • Asking what she can answer herself (her timezone, her currency).
  • Forcing her to set up the kids before she sees value.

→ Design decisions she drives

  • Magic-link auth, no password. One round-trip wins over two.
  • Smart defaults: timezone + currency auto-detected, slug derived from family name.
  • Defer kid setup to in-dashboard cards, not signup gates.
  • Dashboard shows her view of everything — kid drill-downs, all tabs visible.
Y
Secondary Adult · Dad · 41

Yusuf Khan

The partner who joins because Sarah told him to.

"Send me the link. I'll log in once. After that, just notify me when there's something I actually need to do."

Top jobs he hires Family Hub to do

  • See his own pending tasks in one glance ("Fixing the bike")
  • Check the family calendar before booking work travel
  • Approve kid reward redemptions without a meeting about it

Friction he will not tolerate

  • Being forced to set up the family — Sarah already did it.
  • Notification spam for things that don't need him.
  • "Confirm your role" / "set your preferences" pre-screens.

→ Design decisions he drives

  • One-click invite acceptance — he clicks, he's in. No re-entering family details.
  • Notification scoping: only @-mentions + items assigned to him.
  • "My Tasks" tab as the entry point — same chrome as Sarah's view but defaulted to his slice.
  • Magic-link login persists — no re-auth on every visit.
A
Kid Teen · 14

Amina Khan

The teen who is half-on-board, half-rolling-her-eyes.

"Fine, I'll do it. But I want my own space — I don't want my little brother seeing my reading log. And the stickers had better mean something."

Top jobs she hires Family Hub to do

  • See her own habits + streaks — feel proud of an 8-day streak
  • Spend earned stickers on rewards that she actually wants
  • Get reminders without a parent texting her again

Friction she will not tolerate

  • Anything that feels like a kid's app — no babyish copy.
  • Her board mixed with Ibrahim's. She wants her own world.
  • Stickers that don't redeem for anything real.

→ Design decisions she drives

  • Split login: kid login = pick avatar + PIN. No email required.
  • Per-kid dashboard scope: Amina sees only Amina's data — no family-wide tabs visible.
  • Reward shop with parent approval — stickers redeem for negotiated real-life things.
  • Tone-neutral copy on her surfaces. Bold + clean, not cutesy.
I
Kid Child · 6

Ibrahim Khan

The young child who can read, sort of, and loves stars.

"Where's my star? Did I get a star? Mum, can I have screen time for stars? I want the ice cream one!"

Top jobs he hires Family Hub to do

  • Tap the thing he did + watch a star fly into his wallet
  • See his star balance go up over the week
  • Beg for ice cream at 122 stars and feel pride that he earned it

Friction he will not tolerate

  • Reading-heavy screens. Icons + giant numbers + colour beats text.
  • Slow feedback. He needs the star animation NOW.
  • Forms. Anything that requires typing more than his PIN.

→ Design decisions he drives

  • Avatar + PIN login — he picks his face, types 4 digits.
  • Big tap targets — minimum 56px. Habit cards are tappable cards, not list rows.
  • Instant celebratory feedback — star animation on every habit completion.
  • Icon-led copy — every action has an emoji or pictogram. Text is supportive, not primary.

Cross-cutting principles

What every persona has in common — the floor every screen must hit.

⚡ Fast in, fast out

Sarah is impatient. Yusuf wants to leave. Amina has homework. Ibrahim is 6. Nobody wants to be in the app longer than they have to. Optimise for time-to-value, not time-on-page.

🪟 Right view per role

Parents see everything. Kids see only their own world. Never mix surfaces. Role-gated tabs are not a feature — they're the contract.

🎯 Smart defaults > questions

Auto-detect what you can. Pre-fill what you can guess. Reserve forms for things only the human can answer. An empty form is a tax.

⭐ Reward, don't nag

Every habit done is a celebration. Every task closed is a green tick. The app's job is to make finishing things feel good — not to remind people they haven't.

🏠 Built for the family, not the user

Most apps optimise for one user at a time. Family Hub is a shared tool. Designs must show whose data is whose, who's watching, who's responsible.

🖥 Desktop-first responsive

Parents coordinate on a laptop. Kids might use a tablet at the kitchen table. 1280w default. Reflow at 768w only when it changes meaning. No mobile app right now.

When personas conflict — who wins?

Design-system anchors (don't deviate)

Style

Neo-brutalist. 2px solid black borders. Hard offset shadows (1–6px black, no soft drops). Sharp corners or rounded-md max — never rounded-2xl.

Colour

Page bg #3d1065 kingdom purple. Cards white or pastel (yellow-100, pink-100, cyan-100, lime-100, purple-200). Accents from Tailwind 100–700 only.

Type

Headings: Fredoka One. Body: Nunito (400/600/700/800/900). Both Google Fonts. Bold weights everywhere.
