Persona 4 — The Invitee / Family Member

The spouse, kid, or grandparent who got pulled in by someone else's invite

Motivation: stay connected without extra effort Friction: "I didn't sign up — why should I care?"

Examples: Yusuf (12, kid), Amira (spouse, always busy), Grandma Fatima (not tech-savvy). They range from a 12-year-old to a grandparent — the flow must work for all of them.

🚨

This persona's first 60 seconds determines Persona 1's retention.

Every paying parent (Persona 1) needs to onboard 2–5 family members for Family Hub to deliver value. If the invite-to-active-member flow is broken, confusing, or has gaps — the parent churns. Every BACKLOG GAP below is a direct churn risk for paying customers.

Gaps & Open Questions — Persona 4 is the most consequential backlog

Every gap below blocks a family member from completing their first session. That blocks the paying parent from seeing value. That causes churn.

⚠ BACKLOG GAP Invite-aware signup flow (Screens 1–3)
No ticket scoped for branded invite email, invite-token landing page with family colour/name, or pre-filled account creation form. FHS-90 (Sprint 5) covers invite sending but not the invitee-side experience. Risk: invitees land on a generic signup page with no family context — they won't know why they're signing up.
Nearest refs: FHS-90, FHS-97 (Sprint 5)
⚠ BACKLOG GAP First-launch tour (Screens 5–7)
No onboarding tour is scoped. Invitees who join via invite (especially grandparents or young kids) hit a blank dashboard with no guidance. FHS-23 (Sprint 2) covers the admin onboarding wizard — no equivalent for invitees. Risk: cold-start confusion → app abandonment in first 2 minutes.
Nearest refs: FHS-23, FHS-29 (Sprint 2)
⚠ BACKLOG GAP Push notifications infrastructure (Screen 9)
No ticket exists for push notification delivery (APNs / FCM). Invitees won't know they've been assigned tasks without checking the app manually — defeats the ambient-awareness value prop. Email-only fallback insufficient for engagement. Risk: assigned tasks go unseen → parent frustration → churn.
No ticket — raise against FHS-205 (Tech Debt epic) or new epic
OPEN QUESTION Auth choice for minors / grandparents (Screen 4)
Under-13 users cannot have a Google account (COPPA). Does Family Hub need a "kid mode" login (parent-controlled)? FHS-191 covers JWT middleware but not age-gating or parental-consent flows. Needs a decision before Sprint 5 invite work ships.
OPEN QUESTION "Leave family" confirmation + data retention
Screen 10 shows "Leave family" as a destructive action. No ticket defines what happens to content the member created, whether there's a grace period, or whether the admin (Persona 1) is notified. FHS-104 covers member management admin-side — invitee-side leave UX is unspecced.
OPEN QUESTION Invite link expiry & re-invite
Screen 1 shows "expires in 7 days" — no ticket defines the expiry window, re-invite mechanics, or error state when a link has expired. FHS-90 scopes the invite sending; the edge-case states are unspecced.
